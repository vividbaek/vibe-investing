## What this report covers

China removed the full purchase-tax exemption for New Energy Vehicles at the end of 2025 and excluded EVs from the strategic-industry list of its next Five-Year Plan. This report argues that the move marks the start of a market-driven consolidation phase — the automotive edition of a "subsidy → bubble → shakeout → oligopoly" pattern already seen in Chinese ride-hailing (DiDi), bike-sharing (Ofo/Mobike), food delivery (Meituan/Ele.me/JD), and solar.

The central thesis: China's EV push is less an environmental policy than an industrial flanking strategy that changes the competitive battlefield from the century-old internal-combustion domain (where Germany, Japan, the US, and Korea hold an uncatchable lead) to batteries, motors, and software. Subsidies grew 129 brands; the shakeout is expected to leave roughly 15 survivors, who then discharge domestic overcapacity through exports while pressing legacy makers globally with low-cost, software-defined, autonomous-capable vehicles.

The report deliberately includes a **counter-scenario (Section 15)** that lays out where this hypothesis could fail — autos are capital-intensive traded goods unlike closed domestic platforms, ICE may not vanish as fast as assumed, an emergent mechanism is not the same as an intended master plan, and domestic pain is not the same as global victory.

## Investment framework at a glance

The report frames a US-equity-centered reference portfolio (informational only — see Disclaimer). Names are grouped by role in the EV/autonomy value chain:

- **Autonomous-driving SW & semiconductors (core moat):** NVDA, TSLA, GOOGL, QCOM, MBLY
- **LiDAR & sensors:** LAZR, INVZ, OUST
- **Batteries & materials (LFP / solid-state):** QS, ALB, LIT, LAC, MP
- **US EV makers (survivor bets):** TSLA, RIVN, F, GM
- **Chinese EV survivors (risk asset):** BYDDY, LI, XPEV, NIO, HSAI

The underlying logic: whoever wins the EV race, the suppliers of chips, software, battery materials, and LiDAR profit most stably — the "picks and jeans" of the EV gold rush.
